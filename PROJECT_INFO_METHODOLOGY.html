User Roles & Responsibilities
Six distinct roles, each with a dedicated dashboard and permissions
🧑‍⚕️

Patient

  • Book appointments online
  • View appointment history
  • Access digital prescriptions
  • View & download lab reports
  • Read-only access to own EMR
👨‍⚕️

Doctor

  • View daily appointments & OPD queue
  • Access full patient EMR
  • Create digital prescriptions
  • Order lab tests with priority
  • Receive AI slot suggestions
👥

Staff

  • Manage daily OPD queue (token system)
  • Book walk-in appointments
  • View patient & doctor lists
  • Update queue status in real time
💊

Pharmacy

  • View incoming prescriptions
  • Mark medicines as dispensed
  • Track dispensing history
  • Medicine demand forecasting (AI)
🔬

Laboratory

  • Receive & prioritise test requests
  • Assign technicians; track samples
  • Auto-flag abnormal results
  • Upload PDF reports
  • Notify doctor & patient on completion
🔐

Admin

  • Create & manage all user accounts
  • Department configuration & RBAC
  • System-wide analytics dashboard
  • Audit log & security alerting
  • Hospital configuration (fees, hours, branches)

Clinical Workflow Process
Step-by-step patient journey through the system
Step Actor Action System Output
Step 1 Admin Creates user accounts for doctors, staff, pharmacists, lab technicians, and patients Accounts stored in users collection with hashed passwords; role assigned
Step 2 Patient / Staff Patient books an appointment online OR Staff books on behalf of a walk-in patient Appointment record created; doctor notified; token generated for walk-ins
Step 3 Staff Adds patient to daily OPD queue and manages token status Queue entry created/updated; real-time status visible on staff dashboard
Step 4 Doctor Views patient EMR, records consultation, creates digital prescription, orders lab tests Prescription saved to DB; LabTest records created with priority & status "Requested"
Step 5 Pharmacist Views prescription on pharmacy dashboard, dispenses medicines, marks as dispensed Prescription status updated to "dispensed"; dispensing history recorded
Step 6 Lab Technician Collects sample → processes test → uploads PDF report with structured results LabTest status progresses (sample-collected → in-progress → completed); auto-flagging of abnormal results; notifications sent to doctor & patient
Step 7 Patient Logs in to view prescriptions and download lab reports Patient dashboard shows all records; PDF download available

Security Architecture & AI Features
Enterprise-grade protection and intelligent analytics
🔑
JWT Authentication

Stateless token-based auth; token expiry handling and verification middleware.

👮
Role-Based Access Control

Route and function-level authorization via authorize() middleware.

🔒
bcrypt Hashing

Pre-save password hashing with 10 salt rounds; plain-text passwords never stored.

🧹
Input Sanitization

XSS prevention, HTML encoding, and SQL-injection guard via Mongoose.

📜
Access Logging

All authenticated requests logged with IP, user-agent, method, and duration.

🚨
Security Monitoring

Failed login lockout after 5 attempts; unusual traffic (>100 req/min) detection.

📱
QR Digital Signatures

QR code verification for prescription authenticity and shareable link validation.

📊
AI – Smart Slots

Analyses last 30 days of appointments; recommends top-3 optimal time slots.

🦠
AI – Disease Patterns

Tracks disease frequency, age group and gender distribution for public health.

⚠️
AI – Patient Risk Score

Scoring model based on age, chronic conditions, allergies, and appointment frequency.

⚖️
AI – Workload Balancing

Classifies doctors as overloaded/balanced/underutilized; provides redistribution tips.

📈
AI – Peak Hours

Hourly and daily appointment distribution for staff scheduling and resource planning.
